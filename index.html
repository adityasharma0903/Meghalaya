Meghalaya

Location: One of the Seven Sister States of northeast India.
Geography: Mountainous with valleys and highland plateaus.
Geology: Rich in Archean rock formations containing minerals like coal, limestone, uranium, and sillimanite.
Name Origin: Named by Shiba P. Chatterjee, a geography professor at the University of Calcutta.
Rivers
Garo Hills Region: Ganol, Daring, Sanda, Bandra, Bugai, Dareng, Simsang, Nitai, Bhupai.
Central and Eastern Plateau: Khri, Umtrew, Digaru, Umiam (Barapani), Kynshi (Jadukata), Umngi, Mawpa, Umiam Khwan, Umngot, Umkhen, Myntdu, Myntang.
Southern Khasi Hills: Rivers create deep gorges and waterfalls.
Elevation
Range: 150 m (490 ft) to 1,961 m (6,434 ft).
Highest Points:
Shillong Peak: 1,961 m, in the Khasi Hills.
Nokrek Peak: 1,515 m, in the Garo Hills.
Climate
Rainfall: Up to 12,000 mm (470 in) annually, making it the wettest place on Earth.
Temperature:
Garo Hills: High temperatures most of the year.
Shillong Area: Low temperatures, rarely above 28°C (82°F), sub-zero in winter.
Records:
Sohra (Cherrapunji): Most rain in a calendar month.
Mawsynram: Most rain in a year.
Language
Official Language: English.
Most Spoken Languages: Khasi (33.82%), Garo (31.60%), Pnar (10.69%), Bengali (6.44%), Nepali (1.85%), War (1.73%), Hindi (1.62%), Hajong (1.40%), Assamese (1.34%).
